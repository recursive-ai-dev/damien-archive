Here is the Dark Chronicle character template for the **Knight** class in a Brutal Dark Medieval Fantasy RPG. The search results on vulnerable knights in medieval romance and Dark Souls inform some of the structural choices around bodily fragility, the tension between armor and vulnerability, and the idea that knighthood is often about what the world does to the body rather than what the body can do to the world.

### 2. LAYER NOTES

**Layer 1 — Framing Voice:** The framing options are all documents that survived the character rather than the character themselves. This is deliberate: a knight in dark fantasy is often known through what the institution produces about them (confessions, ledgers, transcripts) rather than through their own voice. The emotional relationships range from bureaucratic indifference (coroner's ledger) to institutional guilt (heresy trial) to personal collapse (field surgeon's notes). Each frame disciplines what can be known — a confession admits interiority but may be unreliable; a ledger is factual but hollow.

**Layer 2 — Identity & Origin:** The lineage options all involve houses or institutions that failed their knights rather than knights failing their houses. This inverts the usual chivalric romance and grounds the character in a world where the structures of knighthood are already broken. The armor provenance is nested because the source of the armor is always a wound — someone died in it, someone gave it under duress, someone lied about how it was acquired.

**Layer 3 — Physical Presence:** The build options avoid "heroic" descriptors. The "weathered past age into something geological" line is borrowed from the tradition of vulnerable knights in medieval romance — Havelok and Bevis, whose bodies are open to harm and whose strength comes from community rather than solitary invincibility.  The tremor and the missing armor piece are both details that imply history without explaining it, leaving the GM or writer room to discover the story.

**Layer 4 — The Wound:** The wound is always about a failure of the institution that knighthood is supposed to serve. This is the core dark fantasy move: the knight's tragedy is not personal failure but structural betrayal. The "victory that cost more than the defeat" option is particularly important — it captures the Dark Souls sense that holding ground can be worse than losing it. 

**Layer 5 — Psychology & Belief:** The self-conception options ("tool that has outlasted its purpose," "debt not yet paid," "exactly what circumstances required") are all ways of saying the character has no future tense. The refusal to harm children is not sentimental — it is a fixed point that tests the world around them. The shifting threshold is a nested wildcard that produces different moral architectures depending on resolution.

**Layer 6 — Capability & Method:** The primary capabilities are all non-combat or pre-combat. The "patience confused for stillness" and "negotiation through implication" options give the knight a social and psychological dimension that prevents them from being a stat block. The method — making peace with plan failure before beginning — is the psychological signature of someone who has already survived too many surprises.

**Layer 7 — Legend & Consequence:** The legend options all involve survival that cannot be explained, which is the dark fantasy knight's defining feature — not that they are invincible, but that they are inexplicably still here. The "what they will leave" options cover both death and survival with equal weight, and the survival options are not redemptive — they are about becoming the thing you fought, or discovering you had more to become than you believed, which is not the same as being saved.

### 3. COHERENCE NOTE

**Potential tension:** The "will not harm children" refusal and the "capable of great tenderness" contradiction could, in some combinations, produce a character who seems too soft for the brutal setting. The bridging language is the phrase "for reasons they do not explain" — the refusal is absolute but opaque, and the tenderness is described as a "leak" rather than a feature. This keeps the character's softness from becoming sentimentality; it is a failure of containment, not a virtue.

**Potential tension:** The "built like labor rather than combat" physical option combined with "extraordinarily good at violence" could seem contradictory. The bridge is in the method — the violence is pre-violence, assessment and positioning, which does not require a warrior's physique. The body remembers class, and a laborer's body that learned to fight is a different instrument than a noble's.

**Potential tension:** The "faith in the order that trained them" wound option combined with "will not work for organized religion" could produce a character who seems merely bitter. The bridge is the diagnosis framing — "not as a principle but as a diagnosis," which implies the refusal is empirical, not ideological. They have tried, and the result is always the same. This keeps the character from being a caricature of disillusionment.

### 4. RESOLVED EXAMPLES

**Example A — The Surgeon's Ledger**

*Framing:* A field surgeon's notes from a campaign that was officially declared a victory, though no one returned.

*Resolved:* They are called **The Knight of Drowned Oaths**, and they came from **the last generation of a border house that produced three knights for a war that needed thirty and was erased from the genealogies**. Their armor was **inherited from a relative who died wearing it, the dents never fully hammered out**. They do not speak of where the metal came from, and when asked, they **touch the breastplate as if checking for a heartbeat**.

They are **built like someone who used to be larger and the world wore them down, the armor now fitting loosely where muscle once held it**. Their face has been **weathered past age into something geological, the features eroded by exposure rather than expression**. Their eyes carry **the pale, over-focused quality of someone who sees the room in exits before they see it in people**. They move **with the deliberate economy of someone who has learned that speed is a luxury and stillness is a weapon**. There is **a faint, persistent tremor in the left hand that they do not acknowledge, the gauntlet hiding it but not stopping it**. The armor is **always worn, even where custom would permit its removal, as if the body beneath were something that required containment**.

They became what they are because **they watched the destruction of a company they had sworn to protect, the enemy arriving not as an army but as a decision made by someone who never saw the field**. It took **their faith in the order that trained them, which they had believed was the one institution that would not fail**. What remains is **a systematic distrust of exactly the kind of people who were once their own kind — the well-fed, the well-armed, the well-intentioned**. They have **developed a complete philosophy around it to avoid having to feel it directly, the philosophy holding up better than the feeling ever did**.

They believe **the world is not cruel, merely indifferent, which they find easier because it does not require a villain**. Their operating assumption is that **nothing good survives contact with institutions, especially the ones that claim to protect**. They think of themselves as **a tool that has outlasted its purpose, still sharp but with no hand left to wield it**. They will not **work for the nobility, not as a principle but as a diagnosis — they have tried, and the result is always the same**. The contradiction at their center is **that they are capable of great tenderness and have decided it is a liability, though the tenderness leaks out in small, unguarded economies**.

They are extraordinarily good at **staying alive in situations that are statistically unsurvivable, which they attribute to simple arithmetic: they have survived before, therefore the probability is not zero**. Their primary skill is **the kind of patience that other people confuse for stillness, the patience of someone who has learned that most problems will solve themselves if you do not interrupt them**. When they work, they **say less than is comfortable and wait for the silence to do something, which it usually does**. Among people in their line of work, they are known for **a specific quality of professional honesty that makes them both trustworthy and dangerous, because they will tell you exactly what they will and will not do**.

The story most commonly told is that they **once walked out of a situation that killed everyone else involved, and have not explained how, the explanation having become more valuable than the truth**. They also **made an enemy of an institution that has since found it more practical to pretend the conflict never happened, though the pretense requires constant maintenance**. What actually happened is **simpler and more human than the legend, the heroism being mostly the absence of alternatives**. If they die before this story ends, they will leave **a reputation that will be immediately revised by people who feared them, the revision being the final violence**. If they survive, they will **spend the rest of their life managing the consequences of what they do in this story, the management being the real sentence**.

**Example B — The Sealed Confession**

*Framing:* A sealed confession found in the saddlebag of a dead courier, addressed to a name that has been scratched out.

*Resolved:* They are called **Ser Aldric the Hollow**, and they came from **a monastery that trained boys in the sword before it trained them in the word, and was later dissolved by the church it served**. Their armor was **issued by an order that no longer exists, though the quartermaster's signature is still legible**. They do not speak of where the metal came from, and when asked, they **recite the weight of the steel in pounds, as if the measurement were a prayer**.

They are **the kind of compact that registers as a wrongness before the mind catches up**. Their face has been **kept deliberately expressionless for so long the muscles have forgotten alternatives, so that even surprise arrives as a flatness**. Their eyes carry **a darkness that reflects light wrong, as if the light has to travel further to return**. They move **too quietly for their size, which is the part people remember later, when they are trying to explain why they did not hear them approach**. There is **a habit of standing with their back to walls, even in rooms with no other doors**. The armor is **maintained with a precision that borders on ritual, the leather oiled, the buckles polished, the dents catalogued rather than repaired**.

They became what they are because **they caused a betrayal so precise it required intimate knowledge of their route, their supplies, the exact hour they would be too tired to lift a shield**. It took **their name, which was stripped by the same tribunal that later asked them to serve again**. What remains is **a specific, calibrated anger that they carry like a tool, knowing its weight and its balance and exactly what it will break**. They have **never spoken of it to anyone who survived the telling, the one time they tried resulting in a silence that taught them the lesson**.

They believe **that power is the only honest language and everything else is translation, and they have learned to translate poorly on purpose**. Their operating assumption is that **survival is not a moral achievement but it is the precondition of all others, and they are not interested in moral achievements**. They think of themselves as **a debt that has not yet been fully paid, though the creditor is dead and the terms were never written**. They will not **harm children, for reasons they do not explain, the refusal so absolute it functions as a test of character for anyone who witnesses it**. The contradiction at their center is **that they hold themselves to a standard they would never apply to anyone else, and they do not know whether this is virtue or damage**.

They are extraordinarily good at **violence, specifically the pre-violence — the assessment, the positioning, the one question that tells them everything about whether a fight is coming and who will start it**. Their primary skill is **negotiation conducted entirely through implication, the threat present but never named, the terms understood without being spoken**. When they work, they **commit completely or not at all — there is no middle register, no partial engagement, no trial period**. Among people in their line of work, they are known for **finishing what they take on regardless of what it costs them personally, the cost being the part they do not discuss**.

The story most commonly told is that they **made an enemy of a bloodline that still marks them in ledgers kept in languages they do not read**. What actually happened is **worse than the legend in the specific way that strips out the heroic framing, the survival being the result of a choice that no one would call brave**. If they die before this story ends, they will leave **a debt that transfers, the obligation passing to whoever finds the body, though the terms are unclear**. If they survive, they will **become exactly the kind of institution they have always operated against, the armor outlasting the man until the armor is the man**.

**Example C — The Coroner's Entry**

*Framing:* A coroner's ledger entry for a body recovered from a river, armor too fine for the corpse's condition.

*Resolved:* They are called **Gareth of the Unbroken Lance**, and they came from **a stone village where the only stone building was the keep, and the keep was empty**. Their armor was **issued by an order that was reformed three times since, each time erasing the names of those who served before**. They do not speak of where the metal came from, and when asked, they **say nothing, which is its own kind of answer**.

They are **built like labor rather than combat, the body of someone who carried things before they learned to swing them**. Their face has been **broken and reset by someone who was not a physician, the line of the jaw visible only from certain angles**. Their eyes carry **the flat, patient quality of standing water — the kind that does not move but is not safe**. They move **like they're conserving something that is running out**. There is **a tendency to go very still when the conversation turns toward certain subjects, the stillness so complete it seems practiced**. The armor is **missing one piece — the left pauldron — and the absence is never explained, though the gap is always visible**.

They became what they are because **they survived a victory that cost more than the defeat it replaced, the ground held but the men who held it reduced to something that could not be called living**. It took **their capacity for mercy, not all at once but grain by grain, until the reserve was empty**. What remains is **a grief that has cured into something harder and more useful, like meat smoked past the point of spoilage**. They have **stopped expecting to outlive its consequences, which is not the same as wanting to die, though the distinction is finer than they admit**.

They believe **that most people are doing their best with broken instruments, and this does not make them forgivable, only predictable**. Their operating assumption is that **loyalty is the only currency that doesn't devalue, and they have spent most of theirs on people who did not know the exchange rate**. They think of themselves as **exactly what circumstances required and nothing more, which is a way of saying they do not believe in what they might have been**. They will not **cross a threshold they have drawn somewhere in themselves that shifts depending on what they need it to be, so that they can never be entirely sure where the line is until they have crossed it**. The contradiction at their center is **that they are searching for something they suspect does not exist and cannot stop, the search having become indistinguishable from the self**.

They are extraordinarily good at **reading rooms — not people, rooms: the exits, the loyalties, the thing no one has said yet**. Their primary skill is **knowledge of the specific systems — feudal, military, ecclesiastical — that control a given place, and how to move through them without leaving a trace that registers as movement**. When they work, they **make a plan and then make three contingency plans and then accept that none of them will matter and make peace with that before beginning, the peace being the real preparation**. Among people in their line of work, they are known for **never having asked for more than agreed, which in their profession is remarkable enough to have become a reputation, though the reason for it is not generosity but a belief that asking for more is a kind of weakness**.

The story most commonly told is that they **were present at something significant that history has since revised, and are one of the few remaining primary sources who will not talk**. What actually happened is **something they themselves are no longer certain of, memory being what it is under sufficient pressure, and the uncertainty being the part they cannot speak aloud**. If they die before this story ends, they will leave **one person who understood them, who will not speak of it, who will be fine, the fineness being the lie that holds**. If they survive, they will **find, to their private surprise, that there was something left to become after all, the surprise being the part they will not show**.

Each resolved example is a completely different person: the hollowed institutional survivor, the guilty church-soldier, the mercenary who has mercy left only in theory. The template holds across all combinations, and every combination answers the six quality-floor questions without stating them directly.
