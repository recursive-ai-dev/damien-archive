## LAYER BREAKDOWN

**Layer 1 — Shot & Medium:** The options range from cinematic realism (medium shot, documentary still) to painterly interpretation (oil-on-canvas, digital painting). The medium choice dictates downstream rendering: the cinematic options get lens language (anamorphic, bokeh), the painterly options get brushstroke texture. The "documentary still" implies a captured moment rather than a composed portrait, which affects the pose options in Layer 4.

**Layer 2 — Subject & Core Descriptor:** The physical descriptors are drawn directly from the lore: the "narrowed shoulders" from the annotations, the "broken and reset" face, the "pale, over-focused" eyes. The age options (late thirties to indeterminate) reflect the uncertainty of his timeline — the court-martial was years ago, but the exact duration is unclear. The scar and nerve damage options are specific renderable details, not vague "scarred" language.

**Layer 3 — Material & Costume Detail:** The armor/clothing options reflect his status as a disgraced noble who has been living as a mercenary/survivor. The "once-fine arming doublet" speaks to his origin; the "scavenged plates and leather" speaks to his current reality. The sword options are all battle-worn, none ceremonial — the "notches that might be kills or might be something else" is a lore-embedded detail that rewards close viewing. The scabbard condition (cracked, missing chape, notched) tells a story of maintenance without resources.

**Layer 4 — Pose & Action:** The poses are all forms of stillness, not action — this is Aldric's defining quality. The "tense, predatory stillness" is the pre-violence. The "back to a stone wall" is the automatic behavior from the lore. The "hand half-raised as if interrupted" creates narrative implication: what was he reaching for? The finger details (tracing the pommel, the tremor, the frozen gesture) are all involuntary behaviors that reveal psychology.

**Layer 5 — Environment & Ground:** The environments are all interiors that imply recent violence or institutional decay: the ruined fortress hall (Vosshold), the stone corridor (the court-martial cell), the command tent (the 7th Regiment). The ground details (blood-stained flagstones, drying mud, packed earth) are specific and tactile. The mid-ground details (broken furniture, shadow of someone who was there, hooks with one cloak) all imply absence and recent presence. The horizon options (burning city, storm clouds, grey emptiness) are all apocalyptic in different registers.

**Layer 6 — Lighting & Atmosphere:** The lighting options are all cold or oppressive — no warm golden hour. The "single shaft of cold blue light" is the light of a cell or a ruined building. The "flickering orange of distant fire" is the light of a burning world. The "shadowless gloom" is the light of indifference. The atmosphere modifiers (smoke and embers, freezing mist, heat distortion) all create visual texture that complicates the light and adds depth.

**Layer 7 — Lens & Rendering Finish:** The rendering options range from photographic (anamorphic, documentary grain) to painterly (brushstrokes over photorealistic base). The shallow depth of field option isolates Aldric from his environment, which is thematically appropriate. The macro-sharp option forces confrontation with every scar and imperfection. The anamorphic option adds cinematic grandeur that contrasts with the grim subject. The grain option adds age and documentary authority.

## COHERENCE NOTE

**Potential Tension:** The "tattered, once-fine arming doublet" option and the "arms bare to the elbow" option could produce a character in a fine doublet with bare arms, which is historically plausible but visually odd. The coherence is held by the condition of the doublet — it is "tattered" and "once-fine," which means the sleeves may have been torn or removed. The bridging phrase "the fabric frayed at the cuffs and hem" supports either interpretation: the sleeves are present but damaged, or the sleeves are gone and the frayed edges are what remain.

**Potential Tension:** The "burning city skyline" horizon and the "freezing mist" atmosphere could produce a scene of fire and ice that is visually dramatic but atmospherically contradictory. The coherence is held by the distance: the fire is on the horizon, the freezing mist is in the immediate environment. The contrast between distant destruction and local cold is thematically appropriate for a character who carries warmth (the fire) at a distance and lives in cold (the mist) up close.

**Potential Tension:** The "oil-on-canvas classical composition" medium and the "35mm anamorphic lens" rendering option are technically incompatible (a painting does not have a lens). These options are separated into different wildcard groups and will never resolve together. The medium group and the rendering finish group are independent — a cinematic medium gets lens language, a painterly medium gets brushstroke texture. No cross-group incoherence is possible because the groups are structurally isolated.

## PLATFORM ADAPTATIONS

### MidJourney
MidJourney responds well to art references and atmospheric density. Add these suffixes to any prompt block:
`--ar 16:9 --style raw --s 250`
For the painterly variants, add: `--s 750 --style expressive`

### Stable Diffusion / Flux
SD/Flux responds to weight notation and negative prompt phrasing. Wrap critical terms in parentheses for emphasis:
`((pale over-focused eyes)), ((broken jawline)), ((battle-worn armor))`
Negative prompt: `modern, clean, bright colors, smiling, intact armor, heroic pose, fantasy glow`

### DALL-E 3
DALL-E 3 handles long, descriptive prompts well. Use the full variant seeds as-is. The narrative density of the prompts produces strong results without additional syntax.

### Firefly
Firefly benefits from style reference terms. Append to any prompt:
`photorealistic, cinematic lighting, shallow depth of field, film grain, desaturated color palette`
