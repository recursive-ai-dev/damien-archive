## LAYER NOTES

**Layer 1 — Framing Voice:** The frame options range from institutional (court-martial, recruitment ledger) to intimate (field surgeon's journal, dying priest's confession). Each frame carries different authority: the court-martial knows facts but not motives; the surgeon knows bodies but not names; the squire's testimony is unreliable but emotionally true. The frame determines what layers can be fully realized — a ledger cannot contain psychological interiority, while a confession cannot contain physical detail the priest never saw.

**Layer 2 — Identity & Origin:** The name/epithet options move across gender and social position, from noble scion to foundling to freeholder's child. Each origin implies a different relationship to violence: the noble trained for it, the serf was conscripted into it, the foundling was raised as a weapon. The birthplace options all contain erasure — villages that vanished, towns with no allegiance, cities that are grief. This establishes that the character's world is one where permanence is a luxury.

**Layer 3 — Physical Presence:** The physical descriptions avoid direct adjectives in favor of behavioral impressions. The "wrongness" of their size, the "geological" quality of their face, the way they occupy space "like a debt" — these are all impressions that tell the observer something about their history without the character having to speak. The involuntary details (tremor, wall-standing, weapon-checking) are all survival adaptations that have become permanent.

**Layer 4 — The Wound:** The wound options are structured around the grammar of "they {watched|caused|survived|were the only one who remembered} X." This creates four fundamentally different relationships to the same category of event. The "what it took" options are all forms of loss that are specific enough to be felt: not "their family" but "the only person who had ever understood what they were trying to do." The "what it left" options are all active states, not healed conditions.

**Layer 5 — Psychology & Belief:** The worldview options are all philosophies that a traumatized person might develop to make the world navigable. The self-conception options are all partial — the character thinks they are a tool, or a debt, or something made, but they are also wrong about this in ways they cannot see. The code/contradiction layer is where the character lives: the person who is capable of tenderness but has decided it is a liability is a different kind of tragic than the person who holds themselves to a standard they would never apply to others.

**Layer 6 — Capability & Method:** The capabilities are all rooted in the wound and worldview. A character who believes the world is indifferent develops patience; a character who lost their faith develops the ability to read institutional systems; a character who survived something unsurvivable attributes it to stubbornness. The method options reveal psychology: the person who makes three contingency plans and then accepts that none will matter is someone who has learned that control is an illusion but plans anyway.

**Layer 7 — Legend & Consequence:** The legend options are all slightly wrong in different directions — inflated, deflated, misattributed. The "what is true" options give the GM/writer a secret to work with. The "what they will leave" options cover both death and survival, and each option is a different kind of legacy: a debt, a revised reputation, a single understanding witness, a weapon, a silence.

## COHERENCE NOTE

**Potential Tension:** The "will not harm children" code and the "caused" wound option could produce a character who destroyed something and now refuses to harm children. This is not a contradiction but a causal bridge: the wound taught them something about the cost of harm, and the code is the lesson they extracted. The bridging language is "for reasons they do not explain" — the character does not need to articulate the connection for it to exist.

**Potential Tension:** The "bastard legitimized by war and then cast out by peace" origin and the "disgraced scion of a once-great noble house" option could seem to overlap. They are distinguished by timing: the bastard was legitimized (briefly belonged) and then cast out (belonging revoked), while the scion never stopped belonging but the house itself was disgraced. The scion's wound is the collapse of the structure that defined them; the bastard's wound is the revocation of a belonging that was never fully real.

**Potential Tension:** The "too quietly for their size" movement option and the "stiffness of old injuries" option could seem contradictory. They are reconciled by the fact that both are compensations: the quiet movement is a learned skill, the stiffness is a physical limitation compensated for so thoroughly that the compensation has become invisible. A character can move quietly despite stiffness by developing a gait that accounts for both.

## RESOLVED EXAMPLES

### EXAMPLE 1: The Survivor Who Counts

*Framing:* A field surgeon's journal entry, written in a tent that smelled of copper and rain.

*Identity:* They are known as Mourne of the Saltmarsh. They were born in a fishing village that no longer appears on maps, where the men went to sea and the women buried what the sea returned. They are a serf's child who survived something the serfs don't discuss, and was taken by a lord's levy before the village could process what to do with them.

*Physical Presence:* They carry themselves with the kind of compact build that registers as a wrongness before the mind catches up, the body having been shaped by labor rather than combat, which makes the combat more surprising. Their face is weathered past age into something geological, the kind of face that belongs to a landscape rather than a person. Their eyes hold the flat, patient quality of standing water, the kind that looks shallow until you step into it. They move with the deliberate economy of someone who has learned that speed is a luxury and that most fights are won before the first blow. They have a tendency to go very still when the conversation turns toward certain subjects, the stillness so complete it resembles a cessation.

*Wound:* They survived a betrayal so precise it required intimate knowledge of them, the kind that could only come from someone who had once been trusted. It took their capacity for mercy, the removal so complete that they cannot remember what it felt like to possess it. What remains is a specific, calibrated anger that they carry like a tool, the weight of it familiar enough to be comforting. They have developed a complete philosophy around it to avoid having to feel it directly, the system so elaborate that they sometimes mistake it for understanding.

*Psychology:* They believe that most people are doing their best with broken instruments, and this does not make them forgivable, only comprehensible. Their operating assumption is that loyalty is the only currency that doesn't devalue, and they have spent most of theirs on people who did not deserve the expenditure. They think of themselves as a debt that has not yet been fully paid, the remainder so large that the payments have become a way of life. They will not work for organized religion, the boundary so old that they no longer remember when they drew it. The contradiction at their center is that they are capable of great tenderness and have decided it is a liability, the suppression so complete that the tenderness has become dangerous.

*Capability:* They are extraordinarily good at staying alive in situations that are statistically unsurvivable, which they attribute to simple arithmetic, the refusal to die being a calculation rather than a virtue. When they work, they say less than is comfortable and wait for the silence to do something, the pressure of the quiet having become a tool they wield with precision. Among people in their line of work, they are known for finishing what they take on regardless of what it costs them personally, the reliability so absolute that it has become a kind of threat.

*Legend:* The story most commonly told is that they once walked out of a situation that killed everyone else involved, the walk so unremarkable that they have never understood why people find it significant. What actually happened is simpler and more human than the legend, the heroism being a retrofit applied by people who need their monsters to be extraordinary. If they die before this story ends, they will leave a reputation that will be immediately revised by people who feared them, the revision so complete that they will become unrecognizable. If they survive, they will spend the rest of their life managing the consequences of what they do in this story, the management being the closest they come to a retirement plan.

### EXAMPLE 2: The Noble Who Was Broken

*Framing:* The sealed transcript of a court-martial that was supposed to be burned.

*Identity:* They are known as Aldric the Unburied. They were born in a fortress town that has changed hands so many times it has no allegiance left, only a collective memory of siege. They are the disgraced scion of a once-great noble house whose lands were burned before they were old enough to inherit anything.

*Physical Presence:* They carry themselves with the kind of tall build that registers as an inevitability before the mind catches up, the body having been shaped by someone who used to be larger and the world wore them down, the loss visible in the way their armor hangs. Their face is broken and reset, the line of the jaw slightly off, the nose a flattened bridge that suggests a history of being corrected. Their eyes hold the pale, over-focused stare of someone who sees the room in exits before they see it in faces. They move too quietly for their size, which is the part people remember later, the discrepancy between what they see and what they hear. They have a habit of standing with their back to walls, the orientation so automatic that they do not seem to notice they are doing it.

*Wound:* They caused the destruction of something they believed was permanent, a regiment that was supposed to outlast them. It took the version of themselves they were building toward, the person they were supposed to become dying in the same event that made them necessary. What remains is a grief that has cured into something harder and more useful, the transformation so complete that the original emotion is no longer accessible. They have never spoken of it to anyone who survived the telling, the weight of the unshared knowledge having become part of their physical mass.

*Psychology:* They believe the world is not cruel, merely indifferent, which they find easier because cruelty implies intention and indifference does not. Their operating assumption is that the structures that are supposed to protect people either failed or were never designed to, and that the distinction no longer matters. They think of themselves as a tool that has outlasted its purpose, still functional but no longer assigned to anything specific. They will not harm children, for reasons they do not explain, the refusal so absolute that it has become a kind of religion. The contradiction at their center is that they hold themselves to a standard they would never apply to anyone else, the hypocrisy so invisible to them that it has become part of their architecture.

*Capability:* They are extraordinarily good at violence, specifically the pre-violence — the assessment, the positioning, the one question that tells them everything about whether this will be necessary. Their primary skill is the kind of patience that other people confuse for stillness, the capacity to wait having become their primary weapon. When they work, they commit completely or not at all — there is no middle register, the partial engagement being a form of dishonesty they no longer permit themselves. Among people in their line of work, they are known for a specific quality of professional honesty that makes them both trustworthy and dangerous, the honesty being a weapon that cuts both ways.

*Legend:* The story most commonly told is that they made an enemy of a bloodline that has since found it more practical to pretend the conflict never happened, the pretense being a form of defeat that neither side acknowledges. What actually happened is worse than the legend in the specific way that strips out the heroic framing, the reality being too small and too mean to support the myth. If they die before this story ends, they will leave a debt that transfers, the obligation so specific that someone will be compelled to complete it. If they survive, they will become exactly the kind of institution they have always operated against, the transformation so gradual that they will not notice until it is complete.

### EXAMPLE 3: The Foundling Who Remembers

*Framing:* A confession heard by a priest who was himself dying.

*Identity:* They are known as The Hollow Knight of Drev. They were born in a remote mountain monastery carved from black rock, where the monks trained in violence as prayer. They are a foundling raised inside a military order that treated them as equipment with a pulse.

*Physical Presence:* They carry themselves with a stillness that occupies space like a debt, as if they are waiting for something that has not yet arrived. Their face is kept deliberately expressionless for so long the muscles have forgotten alternatives, the neutrality itself a kind of mask. Their eyes hold a darkness so deeply set that light reflects wrong, as if something behind them is absorbing it. They move like they are always approaching a position they have not yet taken, the body angled slightly, never fully at rest. They have a faint, persistent tremor in the left hand that they do not acknowledge, the hand that holds the shield or the second weapon.

*Wound:* They were the only one who remembered a victory that cost more than the defeat it replaced, the arithmetic of which they still perform in private. It took their faith, the removal so complete that they cannot remember what it felt like to possess it. What remains is a systematic distrust of exactly the kind of people who were once their own kind, the suspicion so practiced that it has become instinct. They have stopped expecting to outlive its consequences, which has freed them to make choices that others would find irrational.

*Psychology:* They believe that nothing good survives contact with institutions, which are machines designed to convert people into components. Their operating assumption is that they are already dead in some essential way, and that this makes them useful for tasks that the living would find too costly. They think of themselves as exactly what circumstances required and nothing more, which is either a description of adequacy or a confession of emptiness, depending on the day. They will not work for anyone who uses the word "justice" too easily, the boundary so old that they no longer remember when they drew it. The contradiction at their center is that they are searching for something they suspect does not exist and cannot stop, the search itself having become the only evidence that they are still alive.

*Capability:* They are extraordinarily good at reading rooms — not people, rooms: the exits, the loyalties, the thing no one has said yet, the negative space where the danger lives. Their primary skill is knowledge of the specific systems — legal, religious, criminal — that control a given place, the expertise so granular that they can navigate institutions the way others navigate streets. When they work, they make a plan and then make three contingency plans and then accept that none of them will matter and make peace with that before beginning, the acceptance being the part that distinguishes them from amateurs. Among people in their line of work, they are known for never having asked for more than agreed, which in their profession is remarkable enough to have become a reputation, the restraint suggesting either discipline or something worse.

*Legend:* The story most commonly told is that they were present at something significant that history has since revised, and are one of the few remaining primary sources who will not talk, the silence being more powerful than any testimony. What actually happened is something they themselves are no longer certain of, memory being what it is under sufficient pressure, the uncertainty being the only honest thing they have left. If they die before this story ends, they will leave one person who understood them, who will not speak of it, who will be fine, the fine being the part that is hardest to believe. If they survive, they will find, to their private surprise, that there was something left to become after all, the surprise being so unwelcome that they will not know what to do with it.

## QUALITY FLOOR VERIFICATION

For each resolved example, the six questions can be answered without being told directly:

**Example 1 (Mourne of the Saltmarsh):**
1. What they want: To finish what they start, to pay the debt they believe they are.
2. What they will not do: Work for organized religion; harm children.
3. What they are afraid of: That their tenderness is a liability; that they will fail to survive.
4. What happened that they cannot take back: Survived a precise betrayal that took their mercy.
5. What they tell themselves about who they are: A debt not yet paid, a person who does arithmetic rather than virtue.
6. What is wrong with that story: They are capable of great tenderness, and the refusal to harm children suggests they still believe in something they claim to have lost.

**Example 2 (Aldric the Unburied):**
1. What they want: To be assigned a purpose again; to hold themselves to a standard they never applied to others.
2. What they will not do: Harm children; engage in partial commitment.

4. What happened that they cannot take back: Caused the destruction of their own regiment.
5. What they tell themselves about who they are: A tool that outlasted its purpose.
6. What is wrong with that story: They hold themselves to an impossible standard, which suggests they still believe in the person they were building toward.

**Example 3 (The Hollow Knight of Drev):**

2. What they will not do: Work for those who use "justice" easily; trust their own kind.

4. What happened that they cannot take back: Was the only one who remembered a victory that cost more than defeat.
5. What they tell themselves about who they are: Exactly what circumstances required and nothing more.
6. What is wrong with that story: They are still searching, which means they have not accepted the emptiness they claim to inhabit.
